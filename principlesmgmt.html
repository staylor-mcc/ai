Start Here: What Do Managers Actually Do?

Many students think a manager is simply “the boss.” In reality, managers help people and resources work together toward a goal. The four classic principles are:

1Planning
Setting goals and deciding what needs to happen.
2Organizing
Arranging people, tasks, time, and resources.
3Leading
Motivating, communicating, coaching, and guiding people.
4Controlling
Checking progress, comparing results to goals, and making adjustments.
Scenario for the whole lesson: Your class has been asked to run a Welcome Week booth. You need a plan, supplies, team roles, good communication, and a way to know if the booth was successful.

🗺️

Planning

Decide the destination before you start moving.

Student version: What are we trying to accomplish, and what steps will get us there?

Booth example: Set a goal to collect 100 student sign-ups by Friday.
🧩

Organizing

Put people and resources in the right places.

Student version: Who does what, when, and with what resources?

Booth example: Assign one student to greeting, one to supplies, and one to tracking sign-ups.
📣

Leading

Help people want to do the work well.

Student version: How do we motivate, communicate, and solve people problems?

Booth example: Encourage the team, explain expectations, and help a shy teammate feel comfortable greeting students.
📊

Controlling

Measure progress and adjust when needed.

Student version: Are we on track? If not, what needs to change?

Booth example: Check sign-up numbers each day and change the approach if students are walking past.
